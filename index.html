[cite_start]

A six-stroke hydrogen-powered CV engine operates on an advanced cycle designed to extract maximum efficiency and extremely clean performance from hydrogen fuel[cite: 178].

    [cite_start]
  1. **Stroke 1 (Intake):** A fresh charge of hydrogen mixed with air enters the cylinder[cite: 179, 181, 199].
    2. [cite_start]
  2. **Stroke 2 (Compression):** This mixture is compressed as the piston moves upward, preparing it for ignition[cite: 183, 184, 200].
    3. [cite_start]
  3. **Stroke 3 (First Power):** A spark plug ignites the compressed mixture, creating a powerful combustion event that forces the piston downward[cite: 185, 201].
    4. [cite_start]
  4. **Stroke 4 (Air Injection & Compression):** A specially designed port opens, allowing a high-pressure stream of air (compressed earlier by a turbocharger) to enter the cylinder[cite: 186, 202]. [cite_start]The piston moves upward, compressing the newly added air and creating ideal conditions for a second power stroke[cite: 187, 203].
    5. [cite_start]
  5. **Stroke 5 (Second Power):** The spark plug ignites the compressed mixture once more, pushing the piston down with an additional burst of energy that significantly boosts overall efficiency[cite: 188].
    6. [cite_start]
  6. **Stroke 6 (Exhaust):** The exhaust gases produced from both combustion cycles are expelled from the cylinder, clearing it for the next cycle[cite: 189].

Unlike conventional engines where all cylinders connect to a crankshaft, this engine uses a **special rod assembly** that synchronises the six horizontally placed cylinders[cite: 194, 195]. [cite_start]This design reduces mechanical losses, improves balance, and delivers smoother power[cite: 196].
