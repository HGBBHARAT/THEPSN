‚öôÔ∏è Mechanism: The Six-Stroke Cycle

A six-stroke hydrogen-powered CV engine operates on an advanced cycle designed to extract maximum efficiency and extremely clean performance from hydrogen fuel. [Image of 6-Stroke Engine Cycle Diagram]

  1. Stroke 1 (Intake): A fresh charge of hydrogen mixed with air enters the cylinder.
  2. Stroke 2 (Compression): The mixture is compressed as the piston moves upward, preparing it for ignition.
  3. Stroke 3 (First Power): A spark plug ignites the compressed mixture, creating a powerful combustion event that forces the piston downward.
  4. Stroke 4 (Air Injection & Compression): A high-pressure stream of air enters the cylinder. The piston moves upward, compressing the newly added air, setting up the second power stroke.
  5. Stroke 5 (Second Power): The spark plug ignites the compressed mixture once more, pushing the piston down with an additional burst of energy that significantly boosts overall efficiency.
  6. Stroke 6 (Exhaust): The exhaust gases from both combustion cycles are expelled from the cylinder, clearing it for the next cycle.

This engine uses a special rod assembly that synchronises the six horizontally placed cylinders, delivering smoother power by reducing mechanical losses compared to a conventional crankshaft.

Emissions

Because the engine uses hydrogen, it produces virtually zero carbon emissions. The main by-product is nitrogen oxide (NOx), which can be easily converted into harmless nitrogen by a catalytic converter.
